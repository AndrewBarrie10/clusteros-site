Common questions

What people ask before commissioning a diagnostic.

What is a stall? +

A stall is a behavioural pattern where a cluster keeps doing something locally rational that prevents it from compounding. ClusterOS identifies nine canonical stalls — including coordinating instead of deciding, mediating instead of coupling, scaling activity instead of improving outcomes, and waiting for permission. Stalls are not failures; they are substitutions that make sense to every actor individually but block the system as a whole.

What is a stabilisation stack? +

A stabilisation stack is two or more stalls that reinforce each other. When stalls combine into a stack, fixing any single one fails — the others compensate and the system restabilises. ClusterOS identifies which stacks are active in your cluster and matches them against verified intervention sets from analogous ecosystems globally.

What is a leverage hypothesis? +

A leverage hypothesis is a specific, testable intervention: a small change that — if it works — shifts the cluster's behavioural regime. Rather than recommending broad strategy, ClusterOS delivers one primary hypothesis per diagnostic: what to change, at which point in the system, with whom, and what observable signal would confirm it's working.

How is ClusterOS different from a consultancy report? +

A consultancy report describes a cluster based on interviews and desk research, then recommends strategy. ClusterOS runs your cluster through a structured 5-stage diagnostic pipeline — Evidence, Patterns, Stalls, Stacks, Leverage — benchmarked against 75 real diagnostic runs across 18+ countries. The output is structured intelligence: named stalls, matched stacks, and a specific leverage hypothesis. It is designed to inform decisions, not fill a shelf.

How long does a diagnostic take? +

A full ClusterOS diagnostic runs in weeks, not months. It is fixed price and can cover any region or sector. The diagnostic does not require extensive interviews or internal access — it works from evidence that already exists.

Who commissions a ClusterOS diagnostic? +

Four actor types commission diagnostics. Anchors — universities and research organisations — use ClusterOS to understand their cluster's stall pattern and build the evidence base for investment. Stewards — development agencies and LEPs — use it to replace generic strategy with evidence-based intervention design. Founders and place-based investors use it to understand ecosystem health before committing capital. Policymakers use it to build comparable intelligence across a portfolio of funded clusters.
